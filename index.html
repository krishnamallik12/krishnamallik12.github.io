CASE 001 / B2B SALES
CRM Sales & Pipeline Diagnostic
Where is $4.8M in sales pipeline actually going, and why?
MySQL Power BI DAX

95% of a $4.82M active pipeline is stale or stuck. The sales team was spending 30+ days chasing deals that would never close.

A B2B sales org had no visibility into pipeline health. Deals were quietly dying and nobody could say where. I cleaned 8,800 records across 4 linked tables and built a risk-scoring system to find out.

Dashboard: Pipeline Risk & Leakage Diagnostic
Dashboard: Executive Sales Performance
  • →It's a closing problem, not a lead problem. Only 11% of lost deals were quick rejections; 45% dragged 30+ days before going cold.
  • →Small, lean firms outperform. Companies with below-average headcount but above-average revenue are systematically undervalued and under-prioritized.
  • →Top agent closed $1.15M, 2× the next best, in fewer days than average. Speed and volume weren't a tradeoff.
  • →Win rates were nearly identical across managers (62 to 64%). The real gap was deal size and account quality, not individual skill.

CASE 002 / E-COMMERCE
E-commerce Funnel & Growth Diagnostic
The business grew revenue 8.5×. So why is it still leaking money?
Python Pandas Seaborn

Paid search drives 67% of all traffic but converts worse than free organic traffic, an estimated $144K in revenue left on the table.

Analyzed 472K sessions and 32K orders across 3 years of session-level data to find where growth was hiding inefficiency.

Cart drop-off, uniform across all 4 products
Conversion & drop-off, desktop vs. mobile
  • →Checkout, not product, is the leak. Cart drop-off holds at 65 to 67% across all 4 products, a <2pt spread, ruling out pricing or fit.
  • →Paid search converts at 6.75%, worse than organic (7.51%) and direct (7.15%), despite getting the most ad spend.
  • →Revenue leader is not the same as margin leader. The top-selling product has the lowest margin (61%) while two lower-revenue products retain 68%+.
  • →Mobile converts at about a third of desktop's rate (3.09% vs 8.50%) on 31% of all traffic, too large a segment to ignore.
